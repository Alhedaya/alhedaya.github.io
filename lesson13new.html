Sun and Moon letters Sun letters and Moon letters are classifications used in Arabic grammar to determine how certain Arabic letters affect the pronunciation of the definite article "al" "ال".

Sun letters are those letters that cause assimilation 'pronunciation changes' of the definite article "al" when it precedes them. When "al" is followed by a Sun letter, the "l" sound of "al" is assimilated into the following consonant, affecting its pronunciation.

Moon letters, on the other hand, do not cause assimilation. When "al" is followed by a moon letter, the "l" sound of "al" is pronounced separately from the following consonant.
