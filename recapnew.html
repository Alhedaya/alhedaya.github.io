Recap of Key Nouraniah Rules and Concepts Throughout this course, you've learned various rules and concepts essential for mastering Arabic reading with the Nouraniah method. Here's a recap of the key rules and concepts covered:

1. Arabic Alphabet:

  • You've been introduced to the Arabic alphabet, consisting of 28 letters, each with its own unique sound and form.
  • Understanding the Arabic alphabet is fundamental for reading and writing in Arabic.

2. Short Vowels:

  • Short vowels, also known as Harakat, include Fat'ha, Dhamma, and Kasra, representing the sounds "a," "u," and "i" respectively.
  • These vowels are used to indicate the short vowel sounds in Arabic words.

3. Long Vowels:

  • Long vowels in Arabic include Alif, Ya, and Waw, representing elongated vowel sounds.
  • These letters are used to indicate the elongation of vowel sounds in Arabic words.

4. Tanween and Sukoon:

  • Tanween refers to the double vowel sound added at the end of a noun to indicate indefiniteness.
  • Sukoon represents the absence of any vowel sound on a consonant, indicating a stop or pause in pronunciation.

5. Shaddah and Madda (Prolongation):

  • Shaddah is a diacritical mark used to double a consonant's sound, indicating emphasis or stress on the consonant.
  • Madda represents the elongation of a vowel sound by adding a supporting letter, usually Alif, at the beginning of a word.

7. Sun and Moon Letters:

  • Sun letters (Al-Huruf Ash-Shamsiyyah) and moon letters (Al-Huruf Al-Qamariyyah) determine the pronunciation of the letters that follow them in Arabic words.
  • Understanding sun and moon letters is crucial for correct pronunciation and reading in Arabic.
